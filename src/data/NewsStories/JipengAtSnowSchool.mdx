# Jipeng Liu at Snow Measurement Field School 2026

<img
  src="/images/news/JipengAtSnowSchool2026/Jipeng9.jpg"

  style={{ width: "100%", borderRadius: "8px", marginBottom: "1rem" }}
/>

**Date: 01/12/2026**

This January, one of our Graduate Student, **Jipeng Liu** attended the <a href="https://www.cuahsi.org/workshops/snow-measurement-field-school-2026" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Snow Measurement Field School 2026</strong></a>, held from January 5–9 in Granby, Colorado. The week-long program focused on hands-on training that connects field-based snow measurements with hydrologic modeling, remote sensing validation, and ecological applications.

Jipeng received intensive training in snow measurement techniques and snowpack characterization while learning directly from senior researchers involved in NASA SnowEx campaigns.

For his group project, he worked on a study that extends his ongoing research in the Cho Lab, examining how forest structure influences snow distribution using airborne LiDAR snow depth data.

Reflecting on the experience, Jipeng recalled how wonderful it was for him to meet people who greatly inspire him on his project. He highlighted how valuable it was to connect theory with real-world field conditions.

> This experience has made me feel committed to integrating in situ observations with remote sensing approaches in my snow hydrology research. Thank you Cho Lab and CUAHSI!!

<div style={{ textAlign: "right" }}>- Jipeng Liu</div>
